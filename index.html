Situational Judgement

This application will test the user's ethical, interpersonal, and resource management skills - skills pertinent to aspiring junior military officers. Respond to the following scenarios indicating (1) the most effective response, and (2) the least effective response. In the end, you find out your personality type in accordance with your selected answer choices.

Scenario 1 - The Salute

Shortly after lunch, you - a first lieutenant - leave the dining facility for the parking lot. As you walk to your car, you see your friend who was recently promoted to captain (a captain outranks a first lieutenant) along with a few other enlisted members. As you approach, you greet with a salute. As you salute, the enlisted members erupt in laughter. What now, lieutenant?


Possible actions:

(A) Laugh along with the enlisted members. Despite rendering the appropriate customs to the captain, it's unimportant to correct the enlisted members.
(B) Call for a meeting with the squadron commander to explain the substandard conduct of the enlisted members.
(C) Make an on the spot correction to the enlisted members. Emphasize how the salute is a courteous exchange of greetings showing mutual respect between members of the armed forces.
(D) Ask to get to know each of the enlisted members personally to understand their behavior.






Scenario 2 - Airmen's Needs

You are the squadron commander at your newest unit at Ramstein AFB - Germany. Your airmen count on you to be a leader and provide them with guidance and resources to succeed. One of your subordinates - Staff Sergeant Smith - informs you of his wife - who is pregnant, days away from giving birth to their child back in the U.S. Sergeant Smith requests temporary leave to witness the birth of his child. How do you respond?


Possible actions:

(A) Delegate the decision to your superior
(B) Listen intently to the sergeant. Devise a plan of action, working with both your subordinate and superior officers, to make an accomodation for the sergeant to see the birth of his child.
(C) Respectfully tell the sergeant that you cannot honor his request; military service often takes precedence over the personal matters of an individual.
(D) Call for a wing staff meeting regarding this matter.






Scenario 3 - Pilot Training

It's May 2024. You just graduated from Lewis University and after completing Air Force ROTC, you comission as a second lieutenant into the U.S. Air Force (your name may or may not be Nicholas Rohmann). You go off to Initial Flight Training en route to becoming a pilot. You're so excited! When you get to the unit, you can't help but notice that the Instructor Pilots (IPs) seem to be passing unqualified trainees on their checkrides. Even some of your peers have expressed their unpreparedness, despite their IPs passing them. What do you do?


Possible actions:

(A) Just ignore this seeming abnormality. You're not an IP. You have faith the IPs are doing their job correctly
(B) Immediately call for a meeting with the wing commander regarding this matter. This is extremly urgent and needs to be addressed promptly.
(C) Confront the IPs directly and respectfully express your concerns on their evaluations.
(D) Consult your peers for their opinions on the matter. Determine if further action is needed in informing your superiors.






Scenario 4 - Family Life

Recently one of your enlisted subordinates has expressed to your their concerns of their family life back home. This airman's mother is fighting an illness in the hospital. Additionally, he feels alone as he seldom reconnects with his old friends and family members back home. He comes to you for your guidance.


Possible actions:

(A) Express your condolences to the airman on illness of his mother and console the airman, but offer no further resources.
(B) Console the airman dealing with his grief. Express that you are always glad to speak confidentially. Additionally, refer the airman to the unit's chaplain, who can provide provide pastoral care ministry and counseling.
(C) Console the airman, listening intently to his concerns. Offer to take him out for lunch.
(D) Refer the airman to your subordinate officer.






Scenario 5 - Being a Wingman

Ever since you've been moved to your new unit, you have noticed that one of your peers - another officer - has been treated unfairly by a more senior officer. The senior officer openly taunts her in front of the rest of the unit, and gives her abundantly more work than the rest of his subordinate officers. You begin to notice that she is starting to get worn out. You can't let this go on forever. What do you do?


Possible actions:

(A) Confront the senior officer in question. Express your displeasure with how he has treated the subordinate officer in question.
(B) Call for a meeting with the senior officer's commanding officer to address the substandard conduct you have observed of the superior officer.
(C) Do nothing. The maltreatment of the subordinate officer does not affect you.
(D) Console the affected subordinate officer, offering your support in getting her through her taks.
